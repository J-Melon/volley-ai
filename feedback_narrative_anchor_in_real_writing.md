A narrative-writer agent generating prose from nothing produces poetry that admires its own image. The fix is to anchor in real human writing **before** drafting: research 2-4 writers whose published prose handles the material the doc needs to ground, cite specific passages, name what to borrow and what to avoid for each.

**Why:** sharpened 2026-05-15 on `designs/narrative/gear.md`. Six iterations across two narrative-writer codenames produced sequentially-prettier prose that Josh called "just bad poetry"; the prose had no human voice to lean on. Annona's research surfaced Mary Oliver, Marilynne Robinson, Wendell Berry, and Kathleen Jamie as anchors, with citations and "borrow this, avoid that" lines. The next narrative dispatch can lean on those instead of inventing voice.

**How to apply:**
- Before any narrative drafting dispatch, check whether anchors exist in `ai/scratchpads/<topic>-narrative-anchors.md` or similar. If not, dispatch a researcher first to produce them.
- The researcher's brief: 2-4 writers whose published prose handles the material; one short cited passage per writer; one sentence on what to borrow; one sentence on what to avoid. Findings to scratchpad. Per `feedback_research_findings_to_scratchpad.md`.
- The narrative writer's brief: name the anchor scratchpad as required reading; state the doc's load-bearing claims; require that the draft borrow image-discipline and rhythm from one or more of the anchors and name which (so the borrow can be checked).
- Per `feedback_named_influences_must_be_load_bearing.md`: when the draft references an influence, the prose demonstrates the influence with researched specifics, not assertion.
- Restart with anchored brief; do not edit unanchored drafts into anchored ones.
