When designing or describing an item, keep the fiction (the object and its character) separate from the design (the mechanic). Do not use the object's flavour as the reason a mechanic works.

**Why:** 2026-05-27, designing Cadence. I kept writing L3 as "the whistle is out of tune, so the rhythm drifts." Josh: "stop saying it is out of tune that is not design." The flat whistle is flavour; the mechanic (an irregular, unpredictable rhythm) has to be stated on its own terms. Same trap as leaning on the helmet "doesn't fit."

**How to apply:** state what changes in play, the speeds, the timing, the triggers, what the player does, not the object's character as a pseudo-cause. The flavour can sit alongside as realisation, but the design must read without it. Pairs with [[feedback_explain_design_in_plain_language]] and [[feedback_anchor_item_design_to_player_goal]], and item-design.md's "Design the mechanic, not the feeling."
