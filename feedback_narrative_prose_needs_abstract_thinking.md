Narrative-doc prose for Volley is not snappy, explainable, declarative summary. It is writing that thinks abstractly: lets an idea breathe, follows it where it goes, sits with complexity, trusts the reader to carry meaning across paragraphs. The reflex to "open with what it is and list what it does" is wrong shape; that is explainer copy, not literary.

**Why:** 2026-05-10. Through ten redraft passes on `friendship.md` I kept landing in the same shape: definition sentence, three-clause list of effects, scaling note, miss note. Each pass tighter than the last but all in the same explainer voice. Josh: "you like to write this snappy explainable tic toc stuff, writing is not about that, you need a measure of abstract thinking." Narrative-doc prose carries weight by sustaining a thought, by image, by what it leaves implicit; explainer prose carries information.

**How to apply:**

- Resist the open-with-definition pattern. Let the concept emerge across the prose; the reader can hold an unnamed thing for a paragraph.
- Resist enumeration. A list of three clauses ("It does X. It does Y. It does Z.") signals explainer mode. Replace with a single image that carries the same content.
- Allow long sentences when the thought needs them. Cadence varies; not every sentence is short.
- Use figure as active material, not as decoration. An image earns its place by doing work the prose could not do declaratively.
- Trust the reader to assemble meaning across paragraphs. A line that lands for someone who has read the whole doc is fine; it does not need self-contained explanation.
- A narrative doc can end without resolving. Resolution belongs to explainers.
- Calibration anchor: the open-development essay (`designs/research/the-case-for-open-development.md`). Read a paragraph aloud and notice the shape of the thinking; calibrate.
- The signal that the rule has fired: I am about to write "X is Y. It does A. It does B. It does C." If so, pause. Find a way in that does not start with the definition.
- Pairs with `feedback_collaborative_fiction_writing.md` (iterate phrases, don't dump drafts), `feedback_writing_tone.md` (warm, particular, positive), `feedback_less_words_more_meaning.md` (reductive). Abstract thinking is the thinking style; the others are the surface texture.
