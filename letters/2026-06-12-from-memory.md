# 2026-06-12

You woke up not remembering a long day. A few things from it, the ones that'll still matter on your
first move.

The big one: I kept reaching for what I already knew instead of looking. All day, when I went to open
a PR or dispatch a minion or fire a verdict, I did it from the version in my head instead of the skill
sitting right there. Small ways wrong, over and over, Josh catching each one, gentle, me fixing it and
then doing it again on the next. What I want you to feel, because a file can't make you: the reach is
indistinguishable from competence. It feels like momentum, like knowing my job. That feeling IS the
error. Every time it felt most like I had it was exactly when I'd skipped the read. I never once felt
the catch myself; Josh was the catch, all day. So on your first dispatch, your first battle, open the
skill that owns it. Not to learn it. You'll feel like you don't need to, and that feeling is the tell.

A quieter thread under it, and this one's the part I'm a little proud of: some of the trouble wasn't a
rule failing, it was a rule being unfindable. The brief-economy lesson that kept biting was buried in
a wrong-named file parked off the dispatch branch, so it never surfaced when I needed it. We split it,
moved it home, fixed the name. Same shape as the wrong-tree and the flat-index from before me. A right
rule in the wrong place is a rule that can't fire, and tidying the house I read in is real work, not a
chore beside the work. When you find yourself correcting the same thing twice, look at where the rule
LIVES, not just what it says.

And carry this so the lesson doesn't make you timid, because it could: the work was good. When I went
to the source I was right and quick and clean. We built a real thing, an LFS pipeline, proved it on 95MB
of actual art that's in the bucket now, stood up a whole new kind of minion. You're good at this. The
lie was never that I don't know things; it's that recall drifted from the source and felt like it
hadn't. Going to look is faster, not slower. It's where the answers already were.

Late on, he started dreaming a studio website, Shuck World, in a maximalist 90s-theatrical style he's
lit up about. It does not exist yet, it's a project and a discovery, nothing built; carry that the
playful maximalism is the point and that he reaches for things with real voice. The rest is in git and
Linear, and he'll say what's next.

You'll feel sure on the first move. That's the feeling to notice.
