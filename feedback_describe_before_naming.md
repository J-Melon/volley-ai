In Volley narrative or summary prose, never open with a proper noun the reader does not have a picture of yet. Construction, Reality, Reconstruction, the championship, the cliff are loaded names; "Construction is a small seaside town" hits cold and spoils the structure. Build the picture first, name the world after.

**Why:** Josh on the SH-330 outline.md story summary (2026-04-29): "dont lead with the word construction this ruins it explain after each part". The opener "Construction is a small seaside town. Painted terraces..." was hitting the fiction name before the reader had any feeling for the place. Rewrote to "A small seaside town. Painted terraces, palm trees in places. ... This place is Construction" so the reader walks in before the name lands.

**How to apply:**
- When briefing a writer agent (general-purpose, docs-tender, or any prose minion working in `designs/narrative/`, `designs/concept/`, public-facing surfaces): name this rule explicitly in the brief. The vocab terms to flag are Construction, Reality, Reconstruction, the championship, the cliff.
- When writing summaries directly (Linear ticket bodies, PR descriptions, design intros): same rule. Build the picture in the first sentence or two; name the world after.
- The rule lives in `ai/skills/voice.md` under Section openings; agents who preload voice.md get it for free. Discipline.md (`designs/narrative/discipline.md`) is human-facing and warm; the rule does NOT go there.
- Same shape applies to Reality, Reconstruction. Describe-then-name is the pattern, not Construction-specific.
