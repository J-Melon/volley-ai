For Volley's protagonist, the call-of-the-void layer is **not** a register of active resistance. It is the daily presence of the pull, alongside the structure they keep choosing.

**Why:** Josh corrected this 2026-04-26 after the cliff-jumping fiction landed. His distinction:

- An adrenaline junkie who jumps from cliffs is **dancing** with the void, not resisting it.
- **Resistance** is the moment of dying when a person realises they do not want to go after all.
- Everyday living with the pull is its own register: not dance, not resistance. The protagonist carries the awareness; the structure (Construction, the rally, the racquet) holds them present; the choice keeps being to stay, again and again.

**How to apply:**
- Avoid framing the protagonist's interior as "resisting the void" or the structure as "holding against" the pull. Both phrasings overstate the friction.
- Use language like "lives alongside," "the pull they carry," "holds them present," "the choice keeps being to stay."
- The cliff is the specific shape of the void for this protagonist: the place the friend died, the place they did not go that day, the place they return to at the end.
- Reserve "resistance" for the dying-realisation register. The dead friend's last moment may have carried it; the protagonist's daily living does not.
- The dance register belongs to the friend group's normalised cliff-jumping. They danced. The protagonist did not. The shopkeeper did, and survived. Each character's relationship to the void has its own register.
