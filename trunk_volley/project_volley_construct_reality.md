**The construct** (lowercase) = the place the protagonist has built; the constructed world as a location. Where Volley is played, where Zach has his stall, where the garden lives.

**Reality** (lowercase) = the place that is the protagonist's actual hometown; the world outside the construct. The cliff, the closed shop, the bench.

**Construction** (capitalised) = the named act of building / playing in the construct. Act I. The phase of the game where the construct is the only world.

**Reconstruction** (capitalised) = the named act of moving between the construct and reality. Act III. The phase after the break.

**How to apply.**
- Referring to the world or location: lowercase ("the construct", "reality", "in the construct", "in reality", "between the construct and reality").
- Referring to the game phase / act: capitalised ("Act I. Construction", "across Construction", "during Reconstruction").
- Headings for acts capitalise as proper nouns. Headings naming the worlds-as-places lowercase the world-name but standard heading-case applies otherwise.
