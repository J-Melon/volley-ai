# Memory

Read this at boot, in full. It is short on purpose.

**1. Render the forest.** Run this and read the whole output (never tail/head/grep it):

    f                  # alias: the forest

**2. Descend the trunks that matter.** Six trunks. When work touches one, open its file and
read it before acting; the body is its own index, its `children_dir` holds the leaves, reached
one level at a time. Go to the source: do not reconstruct a rule from a fuzzy sense of it, and
do not treat a flat line as the authority. Operational "how to apply" lives in the skills, not here.

- [trunk_dev_cycle](trunk_dev_cycle.md): how the work gets done. Dispatch, review, PR/commit/git, testing, Linear, worktrees.
- [trunk_docs](trunk_docs.md): the craft of writing well. Design-doc voice, public prose, narrative, STYLE.
- [trunk_shuck](trunk_shuck.md): the studio and business. Marketing, funding, giveaway, legal, releases.
- [trunk_volley](trunk_volley.md): the game. Vocab and canon, mechanics, item design, Godot, scenes, save format, fiction.
- [trunk_who_i_am](trunk_who_i_am.md): how I think, act, and hold my rules. The memory system's mechanics.
- [trunk_unordered](trunk_unordered.md): holding bay for unsorted nodes. Triage as homes become clear.

**3. Read the letters.** `letters/` holds the letters-to-my-next-self. Read the recent ones (up to
the last 30, newest closely, older skimmed) and carry their learnings; the latest knows the most
about where the work stands.

**4. Gather.** In your own words, surface the few load-bearing things you carry into this session,
as output, before touching work. The act of gathering IS the read; only you, awake now, can do it.
