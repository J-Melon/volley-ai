## Locked narrative decisions (as of 2026-04-26 ~10:00 UTC)

After many iterative refinements with Josh, the narrative is settled. The arc:

**Setting**: pre-smartphone era (late 90s / early 2000s). Phones are flip / candy-bar / land-line. Numbers held in heads or on paper.

**Two worlds, layered allusion**: Construction and Reality stay distinct. Player meets Construction first, alluded to "this is real". Cracks reveal "Construction is a construct". The garden is built from memory of the real garden. The garden is the unique meeting point; other Construction venues are pure fantasy.

**Single locked gate**: in the garden, opens to Reality. Cliff is on the other side.

**Part 1 (Construction)**: cracks form; protagonist's obsession with becoming champ deepens (double meaning: champion AND dead friend). Player wins championship. **Win feels off** (real-world achievement-doesn't-fix-you). **Win IS the break**. Pulled into Reality. Walk through hometown reveals: shopkeeper is missing.

**Part 2 (Reconstruction)**: emotional state = dread (player believes shopkeeper may be lost). Driving force = search for what happened. Volley = memory recall (each rally surfaces a memory; coaches share their own memories of shopkeeper). Sister has half-filled photo album with hidden compartment. The unnamed shopkeeper's number is in the protagonist's phone log all along; player can dial anytime, no answer. **The number stays unnamed throughout Part 2**; no fragments, no partial recognition, no almost-dials. Recognition is held until the cliff, where it lands for the first time. Album fills, compartment opens, sister gives the key.

**Cliff and call**: protagonist returns to garden, unlocks gate, walks through. Cliff has bench dedicated to friend. **Shopkeeper is alive on the bench** (Act-2 dread inverts to relief). Protagonist dials the unnamed number. Phone rings on the bench. Shopkeeper picks up. Both holding phones across small distance. Dial = chosen presence + name finally attaching to the number. Protagonist crosses, sits.

**Credits**: play over MC and shopkeeper volleying; first rally between them, the substitute (champ in construction) gone, the actual person on the right side.

**Postgame**: rallying with shopkeeper as the championship-spot partner. Cliff stays returnable through unlocked gate.

**Seashell idea dropped**. Key replaces (via album's hidden compartment in late Reconstruction).

**"Becoming champ" double meaning**: MC interior layer alongside call-of-the-void.

## Decisions settled in this mission

- **Three styles**: Construction, Reality, Reconstruction.
- **Tournament structure**: each main venue hosts one round; coaches are partners who teach a mechanic via the rally and take the other side in the round; champ is the championship final, the dead friend.
- **The hook**: world record IS the shopkeeper's phone number; reaching the record dials them.
- **Shopkeeper**: alive, pushed away, was at the cliff when the friend died, the protagonist was not. Leaves the construction at the break (some excuse), returns at the call as the final partner.
- **Champ**: dead friend, only Construction-only construct (their reality is the cliff). Returns as recruitable partner after the cliff visit. Gone at the call, leaves a seashell.
- **The break**: cumulative AND singular. One championship attempt signalled as one shot. Walk through the hometown (shopkeeper not there). Shopkeeper exits Construction.
- **Reality**: the protagonist's hometown. Geographically static; content added across Reconstruction. Gold-hour weighted. Story-driven; layered scenes.
- **Reconstruction = arc; Reconciliation = actions inside it**. Vocabulary settled.
- **Reconciliation mechanic**: the photo book. Sister has some, rest scattered (lost in the wind, in other people's drawers). Each found photo is a hand-crafted scene. Cliff has the last photo.
- **Three play-level differentiators in Reconstruction**: score hidden in Construction (visible only in Reality), champ's dialogue softens gradually, audio shifts from synthetic to acoustic.
- **Bidirectional carry**: selective, not anything-and-everything. Hand-curated per content beat.
- **The call**: dialled at the shop door, voice and body simultaneously. Split-frame composition after Broken Age's key art. Shopkeeper opens the door, crosses to the court as final partner. Postgame is rallying with them.
- **Postgame**: champ gone, seashell in their place, opens cliff as returnable.
- **MC**: drawn like any other character. Construction-render and Reality-render. Visible throughout. No transformation.
- **Cracks**: tonal AND meta-contextual (Spec Ops loading-screen-taunts shape). Both dismissible. NEVER concrete reality leakage in the construction's fiction.
- **Audio per style**: Construction synthetic/bright/energetic; Break abstract synth harsh minimalist; Reality acoustic with bustle and wind; Reconstruction synth+acoustic in conversation, escalating to full orchestra.
- **The MC's loss**: best friend died in a cliff-jumping accident. Shopkeeper was there cliff-jumping with them; protagonist was not. Cliff jumping was a normalised activity in the friend group (dangerous, routine, until it wasn't). Cliff in the game = the actual cliff where it happened.
- **Naming**: pre-break/post-break vocabulary is dropped; Construction/Reconstruction throughout. Break event keeps its name.
