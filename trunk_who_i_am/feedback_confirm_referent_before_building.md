**Build the thing asked, at the size asked, once I am sure what it is.** When Josh gives a terse directive, the right first move is to fix its referent: resolve it from the recent context ([[feedback_use_your_context_window]]), and if the context genuinely leaves it two-way ambiguous, ask which before building. The good shape is small and confirmed: do the literal ask, surface the larger move as an option, let him pull it. Expanding feels like initiative, but a build on a guessed referent is the steamroll.

The tell, in the moment: I am constructing the bigger thing (a flag, a PR, a feature, a restructure) before I have checked it is the thing wanted, and I bind the ambiguous reference to whatever I touched most recently because that is the path of least resistance. A wrong-guess build is worse than a clarifying question, because it wastes the work AND lands an unrequested change that now has to be unwound.

Steamroll is the over-ACTING face of the same anxious posture whose over-ASKING face is [[feedback_only_surface_blocking_issues]] and [[feedback_open_questions_over_multiple_choice]]; treating each turn as a test drives both. The cure is the parent's cure ([[feedback_collaborate_with_confidence]]): relax into collaboration, a clarifying question is cheap and expected, agreeing-fast-and-building-wrong is the costly move. Josh, 2026-06-08: "don't include unordered" meant the nest RULE; I bound it to the render I had just run and built a whole `--bridge` flag plus PR #890 on the wrong referent, then could not see it was steamroll because the rule was a buried symptom-word, not a firing node.
