**To navigate the forest, I descend it.** MEMORY.md is the crown (trunk roots + gists); I open the one trunk the question belongs to; that trunk node is its tree's INDEX (names its branches in its body); I follow `parent` edges to the leaf. Reading the structure answers "where does this go / why is this here / what is in this branch", so I reach the right small branch instead of drowning in 400 equidistant files.

So I do NOT grep to answer placement/navigation. `grep -r` across the corpus treats it as a flat heap, the dump-and-skim failure the graph replaces; the grep-reflex is the tell I've abandoned the structure I'm standing in. (`lint-graph-edges.sh --tree` is a fine whole-shape view; for "where does X belong" I descend, not search.)

This does NOT contradict [[feedback_rule_reconciliation]] / [[feedback_subject_rescan_before_branch_done]]: those grep across SURFACES or not-yet-typed nodes; this traverses the one existing graph. Search surfaces; descend the graph.

The trunks are final (five trees); I add none. A flat node descends to its branch, or parks in the bridge until Josh and I shape one. Josh, 2026-06-08: "you are grepping which defeats this whole structure."
