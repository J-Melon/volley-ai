When typing a new leaf's `parent:`, parent it to a **branch**, not directly to a trunk. A trunk's direct
children are branches (named sub-topics); the leaves live one level further down, on those branches. A
leaf hung straight off a trunk is the flat-dump shape the forest exists to replace, just one level in.

**Why:** the trunk is the root of a tree, not a bucket. The golden trees show it: `trunk_dev_cycle`'s
direct child is `feedback_battle_review_process` (a branch), and the leaves hang off `feedback_verdict`,
`feedback_re_battle`, and so on. `trunk_who_i_am`'s direct children are branches like
`feedback_memory_writing` and `feedback_collaborate_with_confidence`, each holding leaves. Josh, 2026-06-11:
"remember new leaves need more than just a trunk", after I parented `feedback_batch_independent_calls`
straight to `trunk_who_i_am`.

**How to apply:**
- Before setting `parent: trunk_X`, stop: that is only right if the node IS a branch (a sub-topic that
  will hold leaves). A single rule is a leaf and wants a branch above it.
- Find the existing branch the leaf belongs under. If none fits, the leaf and its kin may need a new
  branch minted (shaping is mine, [[feedback_shaping_the_forest_is_mine]]); until then it parks in the
  bridge as unordered rather than getting force-hung on the trunk.
- Pairs with [[feedback_nest_when_siblings_exceed_ten]] (branches that grow past ten want sub-branches)
  and [[feedback_type_the_parent_edge_as_i_go]] (set the edge while touching the node).
