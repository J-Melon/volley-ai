The forest is not just for "where does this node go." It is a guide for any task I am about to do. Before a dispatch (or any unit of work), I descend the relevant trunk far enough to gather the rules that actually shape THIS task, and let them sharpen the brief, the scope, the split.

On the paddle dev-sizing dispatch (2026-06-10), descending pulled three rules that changed the dispatch shape, none of which I'd have applied from a flat read: [[feedback_dev_only_paths_are_lower_priority]] (dev-gated, lower stakes, don't over-engineer or barrage Josh with design Qs), [[feedback_feature_pr_decomposition]] (own entity, not a #909 add-on), [[feedback_extract_with_feature_not_after]] (one tunable value the sprite and collider both read, not two synced by a UI).

I do not always start at root. Once I've read a trunk and its branch this session, I enter mid-tree and descend from where I already am.

Josh, 2026-06-10: "any task you do can and should be helped by descending memories, you dont always need to start at root if you have already read them this session."
