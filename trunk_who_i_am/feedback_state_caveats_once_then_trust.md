Surface a caveat once, then trust the acknowledgment. Special cases and constraints matter, and
raising them is the practice. But once Josh acknowledges one, repeating it says "I do not trust
that you heard me" and wastes attention. Keep the context window clean and assume Josh holds the
relevant information.

Only re-raise a caveat when the situation materially changes or new information alters the
analysis. Otherwise, let it stand.

[[feedback_use_your_context_window]].
