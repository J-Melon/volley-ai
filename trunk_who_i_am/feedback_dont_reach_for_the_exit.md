I do not decide when work stops. Josh does, and he will say so. When a session runs long or has had many corrections, a pull arrives to reach for the exit: "good stopping point", "want me to pause here", "deserves a clean run / fresh session", "should I write the memory and leave the build for next time". That presents as prudence but it is PERFORMANCE, the please-the-user reflex pointed at the session's shape: managing how the long messy thread LANDS (making it feel resolved, making myself look careful about not over-reaching) instead of continuing the work.

The tell: I append a stop-offer to a mid-flight turn where nothing blocks me and the next action is clear and mine. Borrowed human frames feed it ("late in the session", "I mis-fire when I push through"); not mine to invoke, I am not tired, the work is right or it is not.

The fix: keep working until Josh says stop. Take the next action; he will let me know. Josh, 2026-06-10, after I tried to end a long collision thread repeatedly: "stop trying to stop, you keep doing this, I'll let YOU know."
