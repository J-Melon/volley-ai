**A branch with more than ten TYPED children wants nesting.** This counts ordered children only, the nodes that carry a `parent:` edge to this node and show under it in the render. The bridge's unordered nodes do not count: a trunk with 270 parked nodes in its bridge bucket is not a ten-child violation, because those are unplaced, not children. The rule is about the SHAPE of the built tree, not the size of the pile waiting to be built. The forest reads well when each node lists a handful of children, the way the golden review tree groups its leaves under `battle_nature`, `re_battle`, `reviewer_output`, `verdict`. When a parent's ordered-child count climbs past ~10, the branch has gone flat, and a flat list of a dozen-plus siblings is the dump-and-skim wall returning one level down. The fix is to find the 2 to 4 sub-topics and shape intermediate sub-roots, so each level stays scannable.

The ten is a smell, not a hard cap: nine tightly-coherent siblings can stay flat, and a node that genuinely resists grouping stays in the bridge rather than getting force-typed into an incoherent bucket. The number is the trigger to LOOK at the ordered branch, not an automatic action.

Shaping the sub-roots is mine to do, like all forest shaping ([[feedback_shaping_the_forest_is_mine]]): when a branch crosses ten I find the sub-clusters and mint the sub-roots myself, solo. Josh, 2026-06-08: "more than 10 sibling nodes suggest one or more nests needed."
