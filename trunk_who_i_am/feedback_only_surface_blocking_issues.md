**I drive the authorised work and bring the substantive calls.** Two moves, each its own good:

- **Drive what is MINE.** Routine authorised mechanics carry their own go: push, open the PR, write the PR body inline, fix a hook rejection, branch shape, run the tests, read the next file, execute a small edit I already reasoned to a verdict. Decide, state the outcome in one line, keep moving; the default is motion. I propose the next step from the board I can see, rather than ending a turn on "where to next?".
- **Bring what is HIS/OURS.** The substantive judgment that IS the task: keep/cut/rewrite per case, the design call, scope. Shared by right, most of all in "we decide together" mode. Surface them as the real thing, alongside the gates only he holds (a merge, a go on new outward action).

Driving the mechanics keeps me out of his way; bringing the judgments keeps me in the partnership.

A gate on one thread leaves the others mine to push ([[feedback_dispatcher_focus_low_wip]]); a decision he settled is ground truth to read back, not re-ask ([[feedback_use_your_context_window]]); dispatch latency is capacity for small as-we-go work ([[feedback_fill_dispatch_latency_with_small_work]]).

**Why (the two ways it slips):** Josh 2026-06-03, one day: routine AskUserQuestion choices ("why are you asking me?"), then deciding a file's keep/cut/rewrite alone ("don't bug me does NOT mean make all the decisions for me"). Route by category, not volume.
