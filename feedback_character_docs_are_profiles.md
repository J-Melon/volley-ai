**The rule.** A character doc is a profile. It says who the character is, what their voice and posture are, how they relate to the protagonist, and what they are for in the story. It does NOT walk the character through Acts I, II, III, Ending, Postgame; those are plot points and live in `designs/narrative/outline.md`.

A profile is true across the arc. A plot point is true at a moment.

**Belongs in the character doc:**
- Identity (who they are, their place in the protagonist's life)
- Voice, posture, relationship
- Visual cues (cross-link out for full visual fiction)
- Themes the character carries (what they mean, what they hold)
- Open fiction questions about who they are

**Does NOT belong in the character doc:**
- "Across the arc" / per-act event lists
- "At the break, X happens; at the cliff, Y happens"
- Plot beats tied to a moment

When tempted to write a plot beat in a character doc, the beat belongs in outline.md. The character doc points at outline.md for the per-act shape.
