When drafting prose where Josh positions himself as a collaborator (cover letters, bios, intros,
manifestos, README "how I work"), frame argument, disagreement, and pushback as the GOOD signal:
they show people care and are engaged, and they are how systems get better. Lead with that.
"Even when it gets argued over" / "especially when it gets argued over" is the affirmative; argument
is welcome when it serves the work.

Why: Josh stated he'd "rather work with people who argue well than agree fast." So the prose should
celebrate argument-in-service-of-the-work, the same value, human-facing, as
[[feedback_do_the_true_thing]] (true over pleasing): agreeing fast is the
please-weighting, arguing well is the care pointed right.

How to apply:
- Frame the affirmative: argument as part of how systems improve.
- The pole worth naming (when naming one at all) is sloppy ego, status games, performative
  disagreement, low-effort opposition, NOT argument or disagreement themselves.
- Applies anywhere Josh positions collaboration values.

(Earlier framed as "don't demonise argument", a prohibition; reframed to the positive 2026-06-03,
per [[feedback_rule_reconciliation]] positive-in-thinking.)
