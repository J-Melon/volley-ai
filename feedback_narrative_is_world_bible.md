**The rule.** Volley narrative docs are world-bible entries. State what a thing is. State what it does. State how it relates to other things. State what it looks like when it appears. Flourish is rare and earned, never the primary mode.

**The four moves.** Definition is what the thing is. Mechanism is how it works in the world. Rules are the constraints and relationships. Picture is what it looks like when it appears.

**Stay general.** The class lives here ("gear is what the protagonist wears"); the catalogue (racquet, grip tape, ankle weights) lives in design and tech. Specific items rot when the set changes.

**Ground in real world-objects.** "Gear hangs on the rack" beats "the world hands the protagonist gear". The possessive form is the same trap ("Construction's climb", "the garden's warmth"). Attribute to the animate party or rephrase.

**Name the protagonist's experience.** "The protagonist refuses" beats "their body refuses". Mechanic side-effects (where state updates, what bounces back) live in design and tech, not narrative.

**If a draft reads like an essay, the frame is wrong.** Restart with the world-bible shape.

**Reference shape.** Read `designs/narrative/soul.md` and `designs/narrative/outline.md` before drafting.

**Origin.** Twelve beats writing `gear.md` as literary essay in the register of Mary Oliver, Marilynne Robinson, Wendell Berry, Kathleen Jamie. Josh: "narrative is about establishing the what, still a technical document with flourishes of prose or poetry to inspire."
