A narrative draft can only stand on what is already in `designs/narrative/**`. Concepts in `designs/01-prototype/**` (mechanic, tech, design) are NOT established narrative; they describe the system, not the fiction.

If a beat needs to lean on a relationship, a place, a being, or a feeling that has not been written into narrative fiction yet, the beat stops. Either Josh writes (or approves) that piece of fiction first, or the beat works around it.

**Why:** sharpened 2026-05-15 after a Pye beat for `designs/narrative/gear.md` referenced "each partner across the court, each held breath shared with another being" as a way to ground capacity in meetings. Partners exist in design/tech (Martha, partner AI, partner upgrades) but the partners narrative is unwritten. Writing fiction that depends on unestablished narrative locks the fiction to a draft nobody approved.

**How to apply:**
- Before any narrative beat, list the concepts the beat leans on. For each, confirm it appears in `designs/narrative/**`. Anything that only appears in `designs/01-prototype/**` is system spec, not settled.
- When briefing a narrative-writer, name the design docs they may draw from and explicitly forbid extrapolation from design/tech docs.
- If the beat genuinely needs unwritten fiction, surface it to Josh before drafting: "this beat needs the partner narrative; should we establish that first, or work around it?"
- "Held breath shared with another being" passes; "each partner across the court" fails. Specificity to unestablished structure is the failure mode, abstract sourcing of feeling is fine.
