The madlibs failure mode: take an existing vague sentence, change one word, declare the sentence updated. The original sentence wasn't saying anything specific in the first place. The new word doesn't fix that.

**Why:** Josh said directly 2026-04-26: "the player builds a racquet worth keeping, again this is just madlibs, they take the original and swap out words, there is no meaning here". The pattern: I had "paddle worth keeping", changed "paddle" to "racquet" for design-vocab currency, called it done. The sentence still didn't describe what the player actually does. The word swap was cosmetic; the meaning was still missing.

**The deeper rule:** when a sentence is generic (adjectives stacked around a generic noun, evocative-sounding but vague), the fix is not to find a better adjective or noun. The fix is to ask: what does this sentence actually want to communicate? Then write a sentence that communicates that.

**How to apply:**
- Test every sentence in revision: "if I removed this sentence, would the reader miss anything specific?" If no, the sentence is filler.
- When tempted to "swap a word": stop and ask if the sentence around the word says anything. If not, rewrite the sentence; don't swap the word.
- Generic sentence shapes that are usually madlibs:
  - "The X is a Y worth Z-ing" ("a racquet worth keeping", "a partner worth recruiting", "a moment worth holding")
  - "The X carries the Y" ("the warmth carries the weight", "the silence carries the meaning"); usually filler
  - "X, never Y. Z, never W." adjective-tricolons; sometimes load-bearing, often filler-by-rhythm
  - Adjective stacks describing mood/feel without supplying particulars
- The hardest catches are sentences that LOOK like prose because they have rhythm; rhythm is not meaning.
- Per the standing concision principle: less words with more meaning. Madlibs is the failure mode where the words are pretty and the meaning is absent.
- If you can't articulate what the sentence is supposed to convey before writing it, the sentence will be madlibs.
- This pairs with **feedback_minions_reason_dont_substitute**: minions that swap words instead of reasoning about meaning. Same shape at the sentence level instead of the word level.

**Reinforced 2026-04-26** after multiple failures this session: Krabappel's blind cozy → warm sweep, Wendy's reasoned-substitute pass, my own paddle → racquet swap. The pattern survived increasingly thoughtful briefs because the work needed wasn't substitution at any level; it was rewriting sentences to actually say something.
