The funding matrix's bands describe how *much* of the game funding builds, not which *parts* of the game are present at each band. Every band ships a full game. The complete arc lands at every funding level. What scales is:

- **Depth in each register.** Volley's two halves are Construction (bright, synthetic, rally-driven) and Reality (acoustic, hand-crafted, scene-driven); Reconstruction is the synthesis register that completes the arc. A band describes how deep each register lands — minimum, working, full — without listing what content is inside it. "Both halves at working depth" beats "five venues and four photo scenes."
- **Polish.** Compact piano versus expanded ensemble versus multi-genre with orchestra; compact commissioned art set versus art-bible standard versus animated and lit. A Band-0 game has compact commissioned work in every discipline; the top band has art-bible-polish commissioned work across the board. **Nothing is "placeholder."** The work shipping at any band is the real work for that band, not a stand-in for a later real version. Piano at Band 0 is the score at Band 0; it ships, it is heard, it is the music.
- **Fidelity.** Compact piano arrangement versus expanded ensemble versus the multi-genre score the audio bible reaches for.

What does **not** scale: which narrative beats are present. Reality is in at Band 0. The cliff and the call are in at Band 0. The break is in at Band 0. They land at compact scope and compact instrumentation, but they land, and the work that lands them is the real work for that band, not a placeholder.

**Why:** Established 2026-05-01 in the funding-matrix thread. Earlier band descriptions sliced the narrative ("Reality entered but thin", "the break landed", "Construction content-complete") as if Reality could be absent at Band 2 and arrive at Band 3. Josh corrected: every band ships a full game; carving narrative beats out of low-funding bands contradicts the matrix's premise that each band IS the full game at that funding level. A buyer at Band 1 is not buying half a game; they are buying the full game built at Band 1's polish and scope.

**How to apply:**
- Scope language uses the register split (Construction depth, Reality depth, Reconstruction reach) and the polish ladder (compact / expanded / ensemble / multi-genre). Both at high level, both without enumeration.
- "Both halves at working depth" lands; "five venues and three photo scenes" doesn't. The funder is buying the shape, not a content list.
- Never write a band that omits or "stages in" a narrative beat. The arc is the arc; it is present at every band.
- Never write any band's work as a "placeholder" or "stand-in" or "rough version" of a later one. The work at any band is the real work shipping at that band; the next band funds *more* and *bigger* work, not the *real* work.
- "Compact / working / full" reads as the depth ladder for each register. "Compact / expanded / ensemble / multi-genre" is the polish ladder. Most bands move both at once.
- This applies to any pricing or funding doc; do not let content lists leak into operational tables.

**Specialists are present at every band; what scales is their scope of work.** There is always a composer; what scales is the score (compact piano → commissioned piano across the game → small ensemble → full ensemble → multi-genre with orchestra). There is always an artist; what scales is the art (compact set → full set → art-bible standard → animation → studio-wide polish). Do not write a band that adds or removes a discipline; write a band that scales the scope of work that discipline produces. Specifically: **never write Band 0 as "Josh hand-drawing" or "Josh playing piano placeholder"** — Josh's only solo discipline is tech (engine, gameplay code), and only at the lower bands. Composer and artist are commissioned from Band 0. Established 2026-05-01: I twice framed Band 0 as Josh-doing-the-work — Josh corrected: all disciplines are contracted or FTE at every band, except tech which Josh handles on the lower end.

**Distinguish specialist commissions from FTE hires.** Specialists (composer, lead artist) are commissioned at appropriate scope at every band; they are senior-from-day-one because they are commissioned for specific work. FTE hires (designers, engineers, producers) follow the junior-talent-treated-well posture and grow with the studio across bands. The matrix's "studio shape" column tracks FTE hires; the "score scope" and "art scope" columns track specialist commissions.
