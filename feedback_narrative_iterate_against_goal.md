A narrative beat builds on the previous beat. If beat 1 picks an off-target frame (a conceit, an opening image, an organising idea), every subsequent beat polishes prose around the wrong frame. I keep mistaking smoother prose for closer-to-the-goal prose.

**The check, every beat:** name the doc's load-bearing claims (the things the doc exists to land). Hold the current draft against those claims. If the draft is polishing a frame that doesn't serve the claims, **stop iterating and restart with the goal restated**. Do not try to fix-by-edit; restart with the goal in the brief.

**Why:** sharpened 2026-05-15 on `designs/narrative/gear.md`. Five beats with Pye polished a "what a racquet weighs" opening conceit, getting smoother each time. Josh: "Bogged down by odd details that don't make sense, can you not see that? We get stuck on what a racket weighs for no reason, what is the goal of this doc?" The doc's goal was to ground three claims (gear is friendship channelled into lift; capacity is accumulated meetings; refusal happens past capacity). The racquet-weight frame served craft, not goal.

**How to apply:**
- Before each new beat, restate the doc's load-bearing claims to the agent. Make the brief say "the doc must land X, Y, Z; reject any beat that doesn't serve those."
- After a beat returns, hold it against the claims. If the beat is doing craft work on a frame that isn't landing the claims, stop iterating; restart with the goal up front.
- Surface to Josh when you suspect iteration is bogging down; do not let beat-on-beat polish hide a wrong premise.
- This is the narrative analogue of `feedback_design_docs_subject_first_github_ids` "cut to load-bearing claims, not justifications." The narrative version: write to land the load-bearing claims, not to admire the prose.
