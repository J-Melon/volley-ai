When a discipline doc lands in `designs/<discipline>/` at the opening-sketch phase (drafted from a single brief, no contact with the practitioner who will own it, no iterations), the doc must declare itself as the team's opening position. Nothing is settled. References, principles, structural shapes, instrument prompts, palette decisions: all are starting positions to argue against. The composer / artist / writer who picks the doc up has to be able to push back without the prose pretending the question was already closed.

**Why:** Putting a doc in the discipline folder usually means "this is settled." But discipline fiction takes contact with the practitioner to earn. A bible written from one chat with one prospective collaborator and shipped as settled overstates what the team actually has. Established 2026-05-01 mid-PR-#562 after I wrote `designs/audio/bible.md` with phrases like "the reference is X" and "the principle is settled; the instruments are not". Josh corrected: "Nothing is settled." The principle wasn't settled either. None of it was settled.

**How to apply:**
- Open the doc with one paragraph naming itself as the team's opening position. Make the qualifier explicit: nothing here is settled, the practitioner picking it up argues with it, what survives the first pass becomes the next version.
- Frame influences as "the references the team is reaching for", not "the reference is X". Add "other reaches welcome" so the practitioner knows the door is open.
- Frame structural decisions as "the opening proposal" or "the opening reach", not "the principle". Reserve "the principle" for things that have been argued through.
- Cross-link the upstream fiction (narrative, art, etc.) honestly: those bibles are further along; when this doc and they disagree, they win.
- The exception is settled the doc inherits cleanly from upstream (e.g. setting comes from narrative; the two-halves structure comes from the game's whole arc). Those facts can land as settled. Anything the doc itself is *originating* is a proposal until argued.
- Once the practitioner has weighed in and the doc has iterated, the proposal-language softens to fiction-language. Until then, it stays open.

**At opening-sketch phase, prefer the high-level idea over the invented specific.** Everything in the doc will change a thousand times before the practitioner is done with it. Specifics invented by the team to make the doc *feel* designed are the first things to get cut. They look like commitments, they aren't, and they are usually wrong on inspection (e.g. "the garden sounds like late afternoon on a stone tile" is not a sound, it is vibes). The doc's job at this stage is the *why* and the *high-level shape*: two halves, references reaching toward X and Y, reconciliation arc, peak at the cliff/call. Per-venue treatment, per-event sting, per-beat instrumentation: those are the practitioner's to answer, not the team's to invent in advance.
