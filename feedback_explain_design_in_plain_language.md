When brainstorming or pitching game-design ideas to Josh, describe them in plain words: what the player sees, what they do, what happens. Name the concrete action and result, not the design principle behind it.

**Why:** 2026-05-27, brainstorming the goop ball's L3 reward. I offered options framed as abstract design concepts ("pays in the opposite currency to the challenge", "a power contact", "see-through", "a different drop"). Josh: "use simple language i don't get these ideas." He is an experienced software engineer but new to game design, so design vocabulary that reads as obvious to a designer is opaque to him.

**How to apply:** say "once you find the real ball it stops splitting for the rest of the round" rather than "it pays in the opposite currency". Figurative phrasing counts too: not "ride it to the top" but "the ball speeds up then slows down, and you hit it when it is fastest". Lead with the on-screen picture, offer to say it plainer, and check it landed. Pairs with [[feedback_describe_before_naming]] and [[user_profile]].
