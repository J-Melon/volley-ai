When Josh gives a terse instruction about a concept ("friendship is like chi, channelled from any source," "friendship is a bond, cannot be between non-living"), the move is to take the spirit of the idea and develop it. Filling in the implications, writing the doc as a writer would. Verbatim transliteration produces stiff, derivative prose that reads as a paraphrase of his chat messages rather than developed prose.

**Why:** 2026-05-10. Josh: "you take me verbatim instead of running with it." On the friendship redraft I treated every chat instruction as a literal sentence-rule and rewrote the doc around each one ("must include 'channelled from any source,'" "must restrict sources to living things"). The result was a doc that read as a stitched-together rendering of the conversation rather than coherent prose. The right shape is to take the ideas and develop them into prose that stands on its own.

**How to apply:**

- When Josh names a concept ("friendship is X"), develop it. Fill in what it implies, name examples, write the manifestations, give it texture. Aim for prose that would land for a fresh reader who hasn't seen the chat.
- Treat his terse messages as raw material, not as authored sentences to embed. Lift the ideas, not the wording.
- Hold the constraints he names ("between living things," "channelled from interests") as anchors, but don't make every constraint a bullet on the page.
- Pairs with `feedback_extract_from_existing_fiction.md` (extract from existing design docs when writing a new one) and `feedback_writing_tone.md` (warm, particular, positive). Verbatim translation violates both.
- The signal that the rule has fired: prose that reads as "Josh said X, then Josh said Y, so the doc says X and Y." If that's the structure, redraft.
- **Comparisons Josh uses in chat are teaching aids, not draft prose.** When he says "friendship is like chi in Dragon Ball" or "like telekinesis in a way," he is helping me grok the idea, not handing me a line for the doc. The doc develops Volley's own language for the concept; do not embed the comparison in the prose. Surfaced 2026-05-10 on the friendship redraft after I wrote "the closest comparison is telekinesis" into the doc. Pairs with `feedback_named_influences_must_be_load_bearing.md` (named influences must earn the clause). Volley docs name Volley things in Volley language; the chat comparisons brief me, they do not appear on the page.
