name: Don't use "final" / "finalised" on Foundation work
description: Foundation work is initial structural setup, not polished/finished art; the words "final", "finalised", "finished" overclaim and rot fast.
type: feedback
originSessionId: ec0dec13-f454-4407-b07d-3f333f30db0f
---
The Foundation projects (Game Foundation, Art Foundation in its earlier life) hold the initial structural pass that downstream production builds on. Tickets in those projects must not describe the work as "final" or "finalised". The output is the floor; alpha and beta passes will revise it. Calling a Foundation deliverable "final" overclaims the polish level and rots the moment a follow-up project lands a revision pass.

**Why:** Josh corrected six tickets (SH-346/348/349 assets and SH-353/354/355 concepts) with "dont use words like final, is is foundation". Each had "Finalised X" in the AC and "as a finalised asset" in the lead-in. Replacements simply dropped the qualifier: "PRODUCE the characters", "Character assets matching the locked style".

**How to apply:** When drafting any Foundation-project ticket, scan for "final", "finalised", "finished", "complete". Cut the qualifier. The work-type label (`asset`, `study`, `concept`) plus the verb (PRODUCE, NARROW, EXPLORE) carries enough; the polish level is implicit in the project being Foundation. Outside Foundation projects (Alpha, Beta, Demo) the qualifier may be honest, but check before using it.
