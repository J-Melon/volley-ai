When a doc's audience is new open-source contributors (item-design criteria, design guideline rubrics, contribution guides), write it kind and inviting. Frame the contents as guidelines, not gates ("a great idea that bends one is still worth bringing"), and drop absolutist phrasing like "never" or bare commands.

**Why:** 2026-05-27, drafting `designs/concept/item-design.md`. I wrote the criteria as terse imperatives with "never a usage multiplier" and "never easier to play." Josh: "dont say never these are guidelines for new open source devs be kind." Volley is open-development; these surfaces greet contributors, so tone is part of the work.

**How to apply:** still concise (see [[feedback_design_docs_concise]]), but lead with an inviting framing line, soften absolutes to tendencies ("the items we love best add...", "tends to help", "ideally"), and keep the door open for exceptions. Pairs with [[feedback_memory_rules_consider_open_source_docs]] and the open-development posture in [[feedback_read_the_essay_first]].
