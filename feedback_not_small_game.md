Do not describe Volley! as "a small game" or "a small project" in public-facing or contributor-facing docs (README, CONTRIBUTING, ROADMAP, marketing copy, grant applications, press material, etc.). Volley! is ambitious in scope: full art direction, narrative, music, multiple milestones through v1 and beyond.

**Why:** the "small" framing undersells the work and sets wrong expectations for contributors, publishers, and Josh himself. It also reads as self-deprecating in a way the project shouldn't lean into.

**How to apply:**
- Phrases like "a small idle game about...", "a small, warm project..." are out. Use "an idle game", "a warm, considered project", or describe scope by specifics rather than size.
- "Small" is still fine when describing things that actually are small in the fiction (the desktop window, gestures, the court as "a small space that feels like everything") or as a technical constraint.
- If in doubt, cut "small" and see if the sentence still works. It usually does.
