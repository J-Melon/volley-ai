When briefing or running research on indie game business, marketing, distribution, or pricing, **diversify sources beyond the two default names** (Chris Zukowski / How To Market A Game; Simon Carless / GameDiscoverCo). Both are real, both are useful, but both have been cited across every Volley research pass to date and the citation pile is becoming monocrop. Josh asked specifically for "deep cuts."

**Other voices to surface first (UK / European bias because Volley is UK):**

- **Cliff Harris (Positech).** UK indie, long-form blog at positech.co.uk/cliffsblog/. Decades of writing on indie sales, marketing, business model, anti-DRM stance.
- **Jake Birkett (Grey Alien Games).** UK indie, has run an indie business blog for 20+ years, GDC talks on indie longevity.
- **Ryan Clark (Brace Yourself Games, *Crypt of the NecroDancer*).** Runs the Indie Game Business newsletter / podcast; data-driven postmortems.
- **Mike Rose (No More Robots).** UK publisher, very public on Twitter / Bluesky / podcasts about indie publishing and what works at small budgets.
- **Tanya Short (Kitfox Games).** Montreal indie, GDC talks on community-led marketing and small-team production.
- **Rami Ismail** (formerly Vlambeer). Community advocate, public on indie support and dev wellbeing; less directly marketing-tactical but speaks to the posture Volley shares.
- **Joost van Dreunen.** Superjoost newsletter; more analyst-shape than practitioner but pulls deeper market data than Carless.
- **Mark Venturelli** (Brazilian indie). Speaks on indie marketing from outside the US/UK frame; useful for non-Anglosphere perspective.
- **Ben Esposito (*Donut County*).** Public postmortem talks; specific numbers.
- **Sean Velasco (Yacht Club Games, *Shovel Knight*).** Kickstarter and stretch-goal postmortems; long-form.
- **Practitioner devblogs that aren't marketing-marketers** but ship marketing data: Anuke (Mindustry), tobspr (Shapez), the Bay 12 monthly posts, Lucas Pope's TIGSource threads. The deep cut is reading what the practitioners actually wrote, not what the marketers wrote about them.
- **Naavik, Video Game Insights, VG Insights blog, GameAnalytics.** Data-side outlets that aren't Carless.
- **Wholesome Games / Matthew Taylor.** Curation-as-marketing case study from a different angle than the standard indie-marketer playbook.

**Why:** Established 2026-05-01. Three Volley research passes (funding-matrix research, marketing-public-good research, giveaway-strategy research) all leaned on Zukowski + Carless as load-bearing citations. Josh: "we are always quoting zukowski are there no other game marketers talking about this stuff? I want deep cuts." The risk of monocropping sources is the same as the risk of leaning on one influence in a character profile (per `feedback_named_influences_must_be_load_bearing`): the work reads as inherited rather than thought-through.

**How to apply:**
- When briefing a researcher for indie-business / marketing / distribution research, name three to five non-default voices the brief expects to see surfaced before allowing Zukowski / Carless as additional supporting sources.
- When citing Zukowski or Carless directly, ask whether a deep-cut voice has said the same thing differently; prefer the deep cut if it does.
- Both names *are* fine to cite, but they should not be the load-bearing source on a claim if a less-default voice covers the same ground.
- Practitioner devblogs (Anuke, tobspr, Bay 12, Lucas Pope) are usually the strongest "deep cut" because the data is first-hand.
- The Volley research scratchpads will get a diversification pass once the new research lands; existing claims that lean on Zukowski / Carless get re-anchored where a deeper voice supports them.
