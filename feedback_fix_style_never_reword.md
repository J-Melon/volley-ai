When Josh says "fix the style", "fix the formatting", or hands back text he edited, change only the formatting: markdown structure, checkbox markup, whitespace, stray invisible characters. Leave his **exact words and punctuation** alone. Do not merge his clauses, rephrase, or "improve" his sentences.

**Why:** On SH-436 he edited the ticket then said "fix the style". I merged his two "So that" lines into one new sentence and reworded the benefit. He flagged it: "you changed the words." The authoring conventions (e.g. [[feedback_ticket_shape]] one-sentence punctuation) apply when *I* draft, not when I tidy *his* text.

**How to apply:** Treat his text as fixed content. Reformat around it. If his wording itself looks off (a doubled clause, an odd phrasing), surface it and offer to change it, do not change it unilaterally.
